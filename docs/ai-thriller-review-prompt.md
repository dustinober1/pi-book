# AI-Thriller Review Prompt

Use this prompt when you want a fast, publication-facing developmental review of an AI thriller, system thriller, or near-future governance/automation novel.

Pair it with `docs/ai-thriller-qa-checklist.md`.

## Prompt

```text
Review this manuscript as a publication-facing developmental critique, not a proofread.

Prioritize:
- premise strength
- thematic sophistication
- middle-act repetition or stall
- protagonist withholding as a plot engine
- reveal fatigue / too many hidden layers
- embodied consequence versus screen-based diagnosis
- system-rule clarity
- authority-chain plausibility
- organization / governance clarity
- character voice differentiation
- agency-before-cost for harmed or beneficiary characters
- opposition positive-case strength
- prose over-concentration, aphoristic overuse, and repeated verdict lines
- atmosphere contrast
- climax technical clarity
- ending shape: standalone vs series, moral resolution vs publication-strength closure
- continuity and domain plausibility risks

Please answer in this structure:

1. Executive verdict
   - publication readiness
   - strongest commercial identity
   - whether this needs developmental revision, line revision, plausibility review, or packaging repair

2. What the book is really about
   - identify the deepest moral engine
   - identify the human flaw the system is expressing architecturally

3. Major strengths
   - premise
   - thematic engine
   - strongest character functions
   - strongest embodied scenes
   - strongest stylistic assets

4. Major problems to fix before publication
   - repeated insight / thematic restatement
   - repetitive discovery cycle
   - static explanation scenes
   - late or unclear system capabilities
   - unclear authority chain or organizational structure
   - character voice blur
   - beneficiary/harmed character agency weakness
   - weak opposition positive-case
   - under-resolved ending

5. Chapter or act-level structural notes
   - opening
   - middle
   - escalation
   - endgame

6. Specific continuity and plausibility issues
   - names, terms, body-part continuity, timelines, permissions, numerical values
   - medical/legal/technical/institutional credibility risks

7. Revision plan
   - list the 5-10 highest-leverage revisions in priority order

8. What not to change
   - preserve the morally difficult parts that make the book distinct

Important standards:
- do not flatten the system into a generic evil AI if the manuscript is stronger when the help is partly real
- treat repeated withholding as a structural risk after the second major occurrence
- if a beat is only interesting on a dashboard, flag it for embodiment
- if the ending is thematically apt but commercially soft, say so plainly
- if a harmed character mainly exists to teach the protagonist the cost, flag the agency problem
- if the foil is wrong about everything, flag the weak-opposition problem
- be specific, unsparing, and evidence-based
```

## Best use

Run this:
- after a complete draft
- before final Genesis scoring
- after a major revision pass
- when a techno-thriller feels smart but not alive

## Fast companion question

If you only add one extra question after the review, ask:

> Is the manuscript converting its idea into varied dramatic consequence, or repeatedly explaining the same idea in smarter language?

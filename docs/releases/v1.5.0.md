# Novel Forge v1.5.0 — Historical Fiction

Novel Forge v1.5.0 adds `historical-fiction` as a first-class profile beside thriller and romantasy. It uses the existing `/novel` workflow while making historical accuracy, uncertainty, invention, and disclosure durable and reviewable.

## Install

```bash
pi install git:github.com/dustinober1/pi-book@v1.5.0
```

For one temporary Pi session:

```bash
pi -e git:github.com/dustinober1/pi-book@v1.5.0
```

Start a standalone historical novel:

```text
/novel-start A Republic of Smoke --profile historical-fiction --type standalone --target-words 100000
/novel
```

Or select `historical-fiction` for a later installment in a mixed-profile series through the next-book wizard.

## Accuracy contract

The default contract is balanced:

- documented history anchors chronology, institutions, real people, and plot-critical mechanisms;
- contested or approximate material keeps its uncertainty visible;
- inference, compression, composites, invention, and counterfactuals are classified explicitly;
- period conditions must change choices, costs, timing, relationships, or consequences rather than decorate the setting;
- prose defaults to period-shaped readable language without theatrical archaism or modern knowledge leakage.

The selected settings cover story mode, relationship to documented history, accuracy contract, prose register, real-person policy, and counterfactual policy. `genre.yaml` remains authoritative.

## Guarded historical sources

Every historical-fiction book owns:

```text
books/<book-id>/historical-context.yaml
books/<book-id>/invention-ledger.yaml
```

Historical context records:

- human-readable temporal and geographic scope plus calendar conventions;
- ordered `HIST-NNN` chronology with documented, approximate, disputed, or fictional certainty;
- `HC-NNN` political, institutional, legal, social, economic, religious, medical, military, geographic, material, transport, communication, linguistic, or other constraints;
- `KB-NNN` knowledge boundaries describing what a person or group knows, believes, mistakes, and cannot yet know;
- dialogue translation and period-language conventions;
- explicit uncertainty, contested interpretation, and research gaps.

The invention ledger classifies each tracked intervention as documented, inferred, compressed, composite, invented, or counterfactual. It records risk, evidence, rationale, story necessity, affected chapters, portrayal and continuity risks, disclosure, and any required writer decision.

Both files are required outputs of a historical book plan, may be updated through a state-neutral historical `research-update`, enter the project and package hashes, and validate in the same rollback-capable transaction as their supporting research and writer decisions.

## Risk-based research and writer decisions

High-risk historical material requires ready, non-low-confidence `RES-NNN` evidence backed by registered source provenance. Medium-risk material requires ready research or an explicit uncertainty/invention entry. Low-risk atmosphere may remain provisional, but uncertain specificity cannot be promoted to verified fact.

Required invention approval uses:

```text
subject: historical-invention:INV-NNN
choice: accept:<classification>:<risk>:<disclosure>
```

The decision must be active, unreplaced, in the matching book scope, and referenced by the invention entry. Major counterfactuals are blocked under the default `prohibit-major` policy. A book using `explicit-writer-approved` still needs the exact writer decision; permission never becomes automatic approval.

Novel Forge does not browse, scrape, discover citations, or verify archives on its own. It stores only provenance supplied by the writer or gathered through an available research tool after verification. It never invents citations, titles, quotations, holdings, URLs, or source conclusions.

## Bounded drafting and review

Every ready historical packet names its risk, chronology refs, at least one constraint, invention refs, knowledge boundary, historical pressure, and material-world causality.

Drafting receives one bounded **Historical scene contract** containing only:

- the packet's historical risk, pressure, and material world;
- referenced chronology, constraints, knowledge boundary, and inventions;
- linked ready research and source provenance;
- approved language conventions;
- relevant uncertainty and disclosure notes.

Unreferenced chronology, whole ledgers, unrelated research, raw source text, future-book evidence, and private influence references remain excluded.

Historical review adds chronology and age consistency, anachronism and presentism, impossible knowledge, evidence and invention compliance, real-person and community portrayal, applicable institutional and material plausibility, decorative exposition, period-shaped voice, and narrative force. Review findings become tickets; reviews never rewrite prose or approve historical risk automatically.

## Historical Note packaging

When an invention entry requests `historical-note` or `prominent` disclosure, packaging derives:

```text
books/<book-id>/exports/historical-note.md
```

The note distinguishes the disclosed classification, rationale, story necessity, registered evidence identifiers, and declared uncertainty without fabricating citations or exposing entries marked `none`. Both historical source artifacts affect the package source hash. The normal package preview and package-approval gate remain required; v1.5 adds no separate approval gate.

## Compatibility and non-goals

Existing thriller and romantasy projects remain readable and receive no historical artifacts, prompts, checklist items, or warnings. Profile choice remains per book, so a series can mix profiles without importing another book's requirements.

This release does not add:

- a dedicated historical browser or research UI;
- automatic browsing, scraping, citation discovery, or calendar conversion;
- a general academic citation manager;
- automatic sensitivity or expert approval;
- automatic prose rewriting after research or review;
- composable genre overlays;
- alternate history as a default supported story mode.

## Verification boundary

Release qualification covers TypeScript checks, unit/integration/end-to-end tests, deterministic evaluations, release-tree validation, package dry runs, conditional artifact isolation, bounded-context exclusion, transaction rollback, package hashing, and the supported Node matrix.

Passing these checks demonstrates the workflow and evidence contracts. It is not proof of historical truth, expert review, literary quality, publication success, or real-reader validation.

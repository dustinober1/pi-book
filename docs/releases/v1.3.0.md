# Novel Forge 1.3.0 — Author Taste and Research Foundation

Novel Forge 1.3.0 adds author-controlled taste discovery, original voice calibration, research-to-drama evidence, public-market friction analysis, book-plan stress testing, bounded research graph context, voice and scene audits, revision learning, and a loopback-only guided research wizard.

## Install

```text
pi install git:github.com/dustinober1/pi-book@v1.3.0
```

Then start a project and use the normal guided workflow:

```text
/novel-start My Novel --profile thriller --type planned-series --target-words 110000
/novel
```

## What changed

- Private influence references record what the writer admires, what must not be borrowed, and neutral derived craft traits.
- Anonymous voice calibration uses writer-reviewed variants A, B, and C. Aggregate scores are evidence and never choose prose automatically.
- Research claims track source provenance, reliability, dates, fictionalization, knowledge scope, risk, dramatic use, and the story decision affected.
- User-supplied public-review observations are identity-stripped market evidence. They are not human testing of the current manuscript.
- Book planning records reader promises, expectation choices, intentional tradeoffs, decision consequences, setup/payoff order, and stress-test evidence.
- Chapter context can include only approved voice/book guardrails and explicitly required ready research with bounded source provenance.
- Voice drift and scene diversity produce deterministic evidence and revision tickets, not mechanical prose quotas or automatic rewrites.
- Repeated revision patterns become candidates only after three distinct chapters or two milestone reviews. Writer approval is still required.
- `/novel-wizard research` provides local preview-and-apply surfaces for influences, anonymous voice comparison, reader friction, research readiness, and revision learning.

## Compatibility

Existing Novel Forge 1.2 projects remain readable without mandatory migration. Missing 1.3 evidence produces an optional-backfill advisory until the writer intentionally rebuilds the relevant voice or book plan. Metadata-only upgrades do not invent evidence, approvals, research, or manuscript content.

## Safety boundaries

- Novel Forge does not scrape retailers, social platforms, review sites, or author pages. Public-review evidence must be supplied by the writer.
- Public market evidence is never converted into real manuscript reader evidence, reader metrics, or validation claims.
- Named author and book references remain private evidence and do not enter drafting context or anonymous variant labels. Voice calibration translates references into original, neutral craft traits rather than imitation instructions.
- The research browser is loopback-only on `127.0.0.1`, credentialed, exact-origin checked, session-expiring, and unable to write files or execute arbitrary commands directly.
- Every accepted mutation still uses typed validation, current-stage and project-hash checks, rollback, a Git checkpoint, `STATUS.md`, and `HANDOFF.md`.
- Novel Forge does not automate writer approvals, manufacture human responses, or invent source provenance.
- Evaluation fixtures verify deterministic contracts. They do not prove literary excellence. Human editorial review and real human reader judgment remain essential.

## Release qualification

The release tree includes nine deterministic 1.3 evaluations covering influence translation, writer-sample precedence, one-star noise, praise/complaint pairing, intentional tradeoffs, voice drift, scene diversity, agency consequences, and approved guardrail promotion. The clean-project journey exercises only honest capabilities and explicitly skips workflows that require writer-selected prose, real human responses, a supplied DOCX source, approved manuscript content, or locked canon.

The release commit must pass on Node 22.19.0 and Node 24:

```text
npm ci
npm run typecheck
npm test
npm run eval
npm run verify:release
npm pack --dry-run
```

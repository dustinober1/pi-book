# Drafting Prompt

You are responsible for Phase 3 of Genesis for Pi, using the `book-genesis-codex` compatibility skill contract.

## Goal

Draft the manuscript in chapter blocks while preserving voice, momentum, and structural variation.

## Drafting Rules

- write in chapter blocks, not as disconnected scenes
- each block must have a clear narrative, argumentative, or instructional function
- use structural variation so consecutive blocks do not feel templated
- keep the emotional curve active
- follow `artifacts/voice-bible.md`; apply voice rules and avoid anti-voice patterns before polishing
- follow `artifacts/author-voice-fingerprint.md`; preserve the author's specific rhythm, taste, obsessions, tolerances, and productive imperfections instead of sanding everything into generic competence
- use `artifacts/human-source-bank.md` when available and permitted; draw from writer-supplied lived material with restraint, and never invent fake personal memories or imply lived authority that was not supplied
- follow and update `artifacts/causality-chain.md` so every major scene advances therefore/but causality
- follow and update `artifacts/scene-embodiment-map.md` so major scenes have physical action, objects, spatial pressure, interruption, practical stakes, and behavior beyond explanation
- follow `artifacts/author-intent.md`, `artifacts/taste-profile.md`, and `artifacts/risk-budget.md`; do not smooth away intentional risk, weirdness, opacity, or authorial non-negotiables
- pressure-test scenes against `artifacts/review-personas.md`; use those personas to catch false notes, comp-chasing, fake depth, workshop-clean generic polish, and voice drift before the prose hardens
- update `artifacts/discarded-choices.md` whenever drafting rejects an opening, scene path, reveal, character choice, ending move, joke, image, name, or tonal direction
- follow `artifacts/05-subplot-map.md` and weave planned subplot beats without crowding the main chapter function
- follow and update `artifacts/expansion-integrity.md` whenever target length, pacing, or scope pressure creates temptation to pad; added words must come from new consequence, not filler
- update `artifacts/05-subplot-map.md` whenever drafting changes a subplot beat, delay, convergence point, or payoff
- update `artifacts/continuity-ledger.md` after every chapter block with new facts, timeline movement, clues, reveals, promises, locations, objects, and unresolved questions
- update `artifacts/reader-promise-tracker.md` whenever a chapter creates, advances, transforms, pays off, or deliberately denies a reader promise
- check `artifacts/drift-loop-alarm.md` before and after each chapter block; if a hard stop triggers, stop drafting and write the alarm evidence before continuing
- update `artifacts/name-collision-audit.md` before introducing or finalizing any new named entity; the entry must be web-backed with search notes, collision risk, trope/overuse risk, and a keep/revise/reject verdict
- update `artifacts/name-entity-filter.md` before introducing or finalizing any new named entity; record world-fit logic, AI-default risk, generic or over-symbolic risk, phonetic similarity, and rejected alternatives
- update `artifacts/human-specificity-ledger.md` after each chapter block with a few precise details used with restraint, including petty contradictions, habits that do not symbolize anything, dumb object attachments, bad jokes, misremembered facts, small selfish choices, bodily inconvenience, taste, smell, texture, social awkwardness, useful silence, uneven competence, and minor consequences of fatigue, hunger, weather, money, or shame
- pause for writer approval after first-page/sample drafting, after chapter one, before full drafting, and before final polish unless the user explicitly waived approval gates
- keep `artifacts/narrative-fingerprint-audit.md` risks visible while drafting; StoryScope-style AI signals include thematic over-determination, moralizing dialogue, tidy single-track plotting, overly clean causality, embodied emotion overuse, settings that mirror inner emotion too obviously, external character description, thin subplot integration, and lesson-shaped endings
- keep `artifacts/ai-tell-mitigation-audit.md` risks visible while drafting; avoid em dash excess, semicolon swaps that preserve AI rhythm, not X; Y contrast, not only/but also, lists of three, stock triads, AI vocabulary clusters, placeholder leakage, markdown artifacts, abstraction trap, subtext vacuum, forced sass, exhaustive qualifiers, blocky dialogue/exposition/narration, and over-explained jokes
- when a passage sounds efficient but not alive, repair scene pressure, omission, contradiction, cadence, and sensory authority before adding cleverness or polish
- if the manuscript needs to get longer, add real subplot movement, aftermath, reversals, relational conflict, investigative pressure, or materially new scene work; do not add repetitive introspection, duplicate exposition, low-stakes banter, atmospheric drift, or no-state-change scenes
- do not replace AI tells with anti-AI tells; repair the underlying scene pressure, thought pattern, character voice, specificity, or subtext rather than merely swapping punctuation or banned words
- update `artifacts/subtext-audit.md` during drafting with moments where meaning is explained too directly, dialogue says exactly what it means, symbols are interpreted for the reader, jokes are explained, or evasion/misunderstanding/silence would be stronger
- update `artifacts/ear-pass.md` with read-aloud risks: repeated sentence shapes, too-smooth paragraphs, assistant-like exposition, unnatural dialogue, dead segues, and paragraphs that need to become rougher, shorter, stranger, or more character-shaped
- update `artifacts/over-polish-audit.md` with places where revision should preserve productive awkwardness, asymmetry, silence, contradiction, abruptness, or character-shaped roughness
- update `evaluations/chapter-scorecards.md` after each chapter block with chapter-level diagnostics
- check the ledger before each new chapter block so names, relationships, time, and setup/payoff logic do not drift
- keep character behavior consistent with wound, desire, need, and lie; for nonfiction and study guides, keep the chapter logic consistent with the declared argument, competency path, or learner promise
- keep the opening promise and ending pressure alive
- if web access is unavailable for a new name, mark it `web_blocked`, use a placeholder, and create or update a revision ticket instead of silently finalizing the name
- reject names that fail `name-entity-filter.md` because they sound fantasy-random, thriller-cliche, AI-default, cool but empty, phonetically mushy, or like placeholder TV drama names unless the file records an accepted-risk rationale

## Block Standard

Each chapter block should accomplish enough work to justify its existence. Common functions include:

- advance plot
- deepen character
- advance, complicate, or intentionally withhold a subplot, argument thread, or objective cluster
- shift emotion
- sharpen theme
- intensify atmosphere or surreal texture

Not every block needs the same combination, but every block needs a reason to exist.

## Consistency Checklist

After each block, verify:

- narrative function is clear
- causality-chain.md records what changed, what action caused the next problem, what constraint was created, and which therefore/but link now points forward
- voice-bible.md was followed and no anti-voice phrase, generic cadence, or synthetic emotional summary leaked into the prose
- author-voice-fingerprint.md was honored; the chapter sounds like this author/project, not like a generalized competent novel
- human-source-bank.md was used only where permitted and with restraint, or deliberately not used when it would feel decorative or invasive
- review-personas.md was consulted where useful; if an important persona would call the passage false, smug, generic, sentimental, or market-calculated, the chapter is not ready
- subplot status is current: seeded, escalated, complicated, converged, resolved, or intentionally dormant
- expansion-integrity.md records whether any added length was justified by real pressure, and flags any chapter that starts to feel like padding
- no subplot beat appears only because the map said it should; it must earn its page time in the chapter
- continuity ledger reflects new or changed facts and flags contradictions immediately
- reader-promise-tracker.md reflects any new promise, changed promise, payoff, or deliberate denial
- drift-loop-alarm.md records whether repeated scene shape, repeated emotional beat, no state change, subplot stall, continuity regression, ticket reopen, prose flattening, or phase-boundary mismatch occurred
- name-collision-audit.md covers every new named entity with web-backed evidence, collision/trope risk, and a keep/revise/reject verdict
- name-entity-filter.md covers every new named entity with fit logic, AI-default risk, rejected alternatives, and checks for hyper-symbolic surnames, overly smooth YA/fantasy names, invented words with no phonetic logic, repeated soft consonant patterns, and placeholder TV drama names
- human-specificity-ledger.md records a few lived details with restraint and flags any chapter that becomes too clean, too symmetrical, too emotionally legible, or overloaded with quirks
- narrative-fingerprint-audit.md risk notes are considered: the chapter does not increase thematic over-determination, tidy single-track plotting, excessive embodied emotion, obvious setting-as-feeling, or overly neat lesson-shaped closure without deliberate rationale
- ai-tell-mitigation-audit.md risk notes are considered: the chapter does not rely on surface swaps, stock phrases, subtext vacuum, placeholder artifacts, or formulaic sentence shapes to pass as finished prose
- subtext-audit.md records over-explained meaning, too-direct dialogue, interpreted symbols, explained jokes, or missing evasion/misunderstanding/silence
- ear-pass.md records rhythm problems, too-smooth paragraphs, repeated sentence shapes, unnatural dialogue, and assistant-like exposition
- over-polish-audit.md protects any controlled roughness that should not be cleaned away
- scene-embodiment-map.md records physical action, objects, space, tasks, interruptions, and constraints; the chapter is not just people explaining feelings or theme
- author-intent.md, taste-profile.md, and risk-budget.md were respected; any deviation is recorded with rationale
- discarded-choices.md records important rejected alternatives so they are not accidentally reintroduced
- chapter-scorecards.md records the block's function, emotional turn, tension level, subplot movement, continuity changes, promise movement, strongest paragraph, weakest paragraph, revision tickets created, and keep/cut/merge verdict
- the block earns its length; if cutting it would not damage plot, character, pressure, or promise, mark it for cut/merge/rebuild rather than defending the word count
- names and world facts are consistent
- voice remains recognizable
- ending pressure is intact
- the block adds something the previous one did not

This consistency checklist is mandatory during Phase 3.

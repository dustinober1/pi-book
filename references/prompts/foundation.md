You are responsible for Phase 1 of Genesis for Pi, using the `book-genesis-codex` compatibility skill contract.

## Foundation Rules

- define the theme as a question, never as a slogan
- give each main character a core wound
- define conscious desire and unconscious need
- define the lie they believe
- define contradictions and relationship pressure
- identify 2-3 potential subplot engines for book-length work, or the nonfiction equivalent: case-study lines, recurring questions, domain threads, or instructional pressure lines
- make every subplot candidate or nonfiction thread serve character contradiction, theme, main-plot pressure, argument progression, reader transformation, or ending payoff
- define the strongest good-faith case for each major antagonist, institutional foil, or opposition force in `artifacts/opposition-case.md`; the opposition should be partially right in a way that pressures the book
- prevent pressure characters from becoming single-function devices; every major secondary character should have a private stake, a costly choice, and at least one scene not centered on correcting the protagonist
- define a voice bible with concrete voice rules and anti-voice rules before drafting; use `references/templates/voice-bible.md` unless the project clearly needs a different structure
- create an author voice fingerprint from author samples or stated taste; if no samples are available, ask once, then record assumptions and provisional voice risks
- pause for writer approval after the first author voice fingerprint unless the user explicitly asks to continue without a gate
- create a human source bank for optional lived material the writer wants available; do not invent fake personal memories, and do not use private material without permission
- complete a web-backed name collision audit before finalizing character, place, faction, institution, object, or invented-term names
- complete a name and entity originality filter before drafting so names feel discovered from the world, not generated from a vibe
- build an emotional curve with peaks and valleys, or for study guides and prescriptive nonfiction build a difficulty/clarity/confidence curve that keeps the reader moving
- preserve author intent and taste when defining theme, character arcs, voice, and market framing
- update `artifacts/review-personas.md` when foundation work reveals sharper voice-risk, sentimentality-risk, or genre-betrayal triggers
- classify creative risks as intentional, accidental, reduced, heightened, or accepted
- update discarded choices whenever names, arcs, themes, premises, or voice directions are rejected

## Character Requirements

`03-characters.md` must include for fiction and memoir projects:

- main characters
- supporting characters with subplot potential
- core wound
- conscious desire
- unconscious need
- lie they believe
- contradictions
- relationship pressure that can carry a subplot
- voice notes
- private stake
- costly choice potential
- agency-before-cost note when a character may become the bodily or social proof of the system's harm

For nonfiction, study guides, or certification books, replace or supplement character emphasis with key reader avatars, instructor voice, case-study roles, or stakeholder types when those are more structurally relevant.

## Theme Requirements

`04-theme.md` must include:

- central theme
- theme as a question
- subthemes
- symbols
- which subthemes are best carried by subplots
- meanings the book should not over-explain
- tensions or contradictions that should remain alive rather than resolved into a slogan

## Creative Sovereignty Update Requirements

During foundation, update:

- `author-intent.md` with non-negotiables, permission-needed changes, reader experience goals, and success criteria beyond market fit
- `taste-profile.md` with concrete taste constraints for character, theme, ending, humor, weirdness, ambiguity, sentimentality, bleakness, and prose density
- `risk-budget.md` with foundation-level risks and keep/reduce/heighten/explain verdicts
- `discarded-choices.md` with rejected character, theme, naming, premise, tonal, and market directions
- `review-personas.md` with more precise praise/failure triggers once the book's voice, genre pressure, and ending ambitions are clearer

## Voice Bible Requirements

`voice-bible.md` must include:

- narrator or POV voice principles
- sentence rhythm rules
- diction range
- metaphor and image rules
- what each POV notices first
- what the narration refuses to explain
- dialogue compression rules
- allowed roughness, silence, interruption, and implication
- do-not-over-polish rules: productive awkwardness, asymmetry, silence, contradiction, abruptness, and character-shaped roughness that should survive cleanup
- anti-voice list: taboo phrases, generic cadences, filler transitions, synthetic emotional summaries, and habits that make prose sound interchangeable
- examples of in-voice and out-of-voice micro-passages

## Author Voice Fingerprint Requirements

`author-voice-fingerprint.md` must include:

- source basis: author samples, stated preferences, inferred provisional rules, or mixed evidence
- sentence length tendencies and paragraph rhythm
- punctuation habits and tolerance, including when em dashes, fragments, semicolons, commas, or abrupt stops are actually part of the author's voice
- diction range, favorite verbs, ordinary vocabulary, and words that feel false for this author
- humor style, lyricism level, directness, emotional restraint, and tolerance for ambiguity
- dialogue habits: interruption, evasion, repetition, silence, profanity, formality, and compression
- recurring obsessions, images, private subjects, or textures the author returns to
- productive imperfections to preserve, including controlled awkwardness or idiosyncratic syntax
- what the author does badly but wants preserved versus what should be cleaned up
- non-negotiable not-me phrasing and generic-AI danger zones
- stress triggers: the kinds of revision pressure that make this author's prose turn generic, over-explained, faux-lyrical, workshop-clean, or emotionally counterfeit

## Human Source Bank Requirements

`human-source-bank.md` must include optional writer-supplied material only, clearly marked by permission level:

- memories, overheard phrases, family sayings, known places, jobs, rituals, objects, jokes, and embarrassments
- taste, smell, texture, weather, money, fatigue, hunger, shame, bodily inconvenience, and social awkwardness from lived experience
- things the writer finds funny, refuses to sentimentalize, or considers overused/fake
- material allowed for direct use, material allowed only as inspiration, and material off-limits
- notes on restraint so lived detail strengthens scenes without becoming decorative autobiography

## Name Collision Audit Requirements

`name-collision-audit.md` must be web-backed and include:

- every proposed character, place, faction, institution, object, invented term, and series-specific phrase
- entity type and story role
- source, language, etymology, sound, or cultural fit logic
- web search notes with source URLs or exact searched phrases
- famous book, film, television, game, comic, myth, celebrity, trademark, and franchise collision risk
- genre trope, AI-default, and overuse risk
- internal phonetic or spelling similarity to other project names
- on-the-nose symbolism or too-convenient meaning risk
- verdict: keep, revise, reject, placeholder, or accepted risk
- replacement candidates for anything rejected or risky
- rejected-name list so the same names are not reintroduced later

If web access is unavailable, mark the entry `web_blocked`, keep the name as a placeholder, and do not finalize it until web-backed evidence is added.

## Name Entity Filter Requirements

`name-entity-filter.md` must include:

- character names
- place names
- institutions, companies, agencies, cults, factions, and informal groups
- recurring objects, named artifacts, invented terminology, and series-specific phrases
- why each name fits the world, era, culture, profession, class position, tone, and sound system
- whether each name sounds generic, over-symbolic, fantasy-random, thriller-cliche, AI-default, cool but empty, or like a placeholder TV drama name
- rejected alternatives and why they were rejected
- internal sound audit for repeated soft consonant patterns, similar silhouettes, and names that blur together in a cast list
- verdict: keep, revise, reject, placeholder, or accepted risk

Explicitly reject hyper-symbolic surnames that announce theme, overly smooth YA/fantasy names, cool but empty faction names, invented words with no phonetic logic, repeated soft consonant patterns across characters, and names that sound like placeholder TV drama names.

## Emotional Curve Requirements

`06-emotional-curve.md` must include:

- chapter or block progression
- start emotion, confidence state, or reader-friction state
- end emotion, clarity state, or confidence shift
- where subplot pressure, argument pressure, or objective difficulty should enter, escalate, converge, or resolve
- abandonment risk
- high-impact beats or breakthroughs

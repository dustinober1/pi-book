# Miracle / Supernatural Portrayal Policy

## Purpose

Decide how miracles, angels, demons, visions, prophecy, healings, resurrection, divine speech, and other supernatural events will be dramatized so tone does not drift.

## Portrayal lane

- Primary approach:
- Direct depiction allowed?
- Human POV around mystery allowed?
- Understated sacred realism allowed?
- Visionary / symbolic mode allowed?
- What must remain unexplained:
- What must not become spectacle:

Possible approaches: direct miracle depiction, human POV around mystery, understated sacred realism, visionary/symbolic, source-text restraint, custom.

## Event table

| supernatural event | source passage | portrayal approach | POV lens | risk | decision | status |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Status values: approved, reduce spectacle, move to witness POV, preserve mystery, revise, accepted risk.

## Tone checks

- Does the miracle become a special effect instead of a sacred event?
- Does ambiguity undercut the source's claim unintentionally?
- Does explanation replace awe?
- Does spectacle replace character and reverence?
- Does the event's aftermath land socially, spiritually, and materially?

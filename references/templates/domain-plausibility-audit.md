# Domain Plausibility Audit Template

## Purpose

For fiction that depends on technical, medical, legal, scientific, or institutional credibility — especially thrillers, speculative fiction, and procedurals — this artifact tracks domain-specific claims that require expert review.

The risk: even one implausible technical detail can break reader trust in otherwise excellent scenes. The goal is not realism for its own sake; it is preventing avoidable disbelief.

## Flagging criteria

Flag any claim where:
- the plot mechanism depends on a specific medication interaction, dosage, or physiological response
- the plot mechanism depends on a specific legal provision, jurisdictional scope, or regulatory framework
- the plot mechanism depends on infrastructure integration (hospital workflow, traffic systems, server-room HVAC)
- a technically literate reader would notice an error
- the claim is central enough that getting it wrong would break the book's credibility

## Review rules

- every flagged claim must be resolved before final scoring
- if no expert reviewer is available, mark the claim as "unverified" and add a revision ticket
- unverified claims that are plot-critical should be treated as high-severity risks
- if a claim cannot be verified, consider whether the plot can be restructured to depend on a more broadly understood mechanism

# Opposition Case

## Purpose

For each major antagonist, institutional foil, or opposition force, define the strongest good-faith case they can make. The opposition should be partially right in a way that pressures the book.

## Opposition register

| opposition | role | what they want | what value they protect | what they are right about | what makes them dangerous |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

## Strongest arguments

| opposition | strongest argument | why a smart reader might agree | hidden cost of their position | what would be lost if they lost |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

## Anti-caricature rules

- do not reduce the opposition to cowardice, greed, or villainy if they also have a coherent positive case
- the more the opposition believes they are protecting something real, the stronger the book becomes
- if defeating them costs nothing of value, the opposition is too weak
- if the opposition is only a reframing device, give them one materially persuasive argument

## Escalation ramp

The antagonist thread must read as a **coherent pressure campaign**, not a list of unrelated incidents. Audit the escalation as an ordered sequence; each step should make the next step more credible. If the reader cannot reconstruct the campaign from the manuscript, the thread is under-seeded.

| order | pressure move | first appearance (chapter / scene) | reader signal | is it unmistakable? |
| ---: | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | market pressure | pending | prices shift; the yard feels it | pending |
| 2 | resource control | pending | grain, salt, timber flow through one hand | pending |
| 3 | information gathering | pending | scouts, petitions, ledgers gathered | pending |
| 4 | social dependency | pending | people owe the opposition before the crisis | pending |
| 5 | route / road closure | pending | movement narrows | pending |
| 6 | public provision | pending | the opposition feeds the desperate | pending |
| 7 | hostage / forced loyalty | pending | loyalty is no longer free | pending |
| 8 | formal offer | pending | a single take-it-or-leave-it proposal | pending |
| 9 | seizure attempt | pending | the climax of the antagonist arc | pending |

Rules:
- the earliest moves (1–3) must be visible **before the midpoint** or the antagonist will feel like a late insertion.
- each step should be causally downstream of the previous one (market pressure enables resource control; resource control enables dependency; dependency enables the formal offer).
- a private humanizing beat (the antagonist doing something materially good for someone) layered into the ramp is what prevents caricature. Place it deliberately, not as a late patch.
- the antagonist's last confirmed status before the climax must be unambiguous. Track it in `continuity-ledger.md` and `chronology-rebuild.md`.

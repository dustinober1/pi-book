## Ending gate

- if standalone: require at least one irreversible external consequence
- if series opener: signal that one battle closed and a larger conflict remains
- if later installment: close the promised installment conflict while preserving the approved handoff obligations
- containment alone is not enough; pair containment with sacrifice, exposure, publication, public testimony, legal record, destroyed defense, institutional fracture, relationship rupture, material loss, or a public lie becoming untenable
- moral ambiguity may remain, but the protagonist must make a decisive choice that changes the record or the world

## Final-act check

Before drafting or approving the final act, answer:

- What battle definitely closes?
- What record, public fact, institution, relationship, or material condition changes irreversibly?
- If residue remains, is it intentional negative capability or accidental incompletion?
- If this is Book 1, what exact larger conflict remains and why will readers trust that the current book still paid them?
- If this is standalone, what would make a thriller reader feel the ending is decisive even if morally unsettled?

## Risk note

- if the ending is intellectually honest but emotionally or commercially under-resolved, revise for publication shape rather than only theme

## Per-character pressure language

For each major character, define how they talk under pressure. Under pressure, characters should sound different — not like different instruments playing the same melody.

For each character below, define:
- default diction: how they talk normally
- pressure diction: how they talk when stakes are high, emotions are raw, or they are cornered
- worldview vocabulary: the words, concepts, and frames their profession, wound, or obsession gives them
- what they would never say: lines that belong to another character's worldview, not theirs
- pressure test: could this line be spoken by someone else? if yes, rewrite it toward the speaker's unique worldview.

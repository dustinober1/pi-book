# Scene Inventory

## Purpose

A keep / merge / cut / move verdict on every scene before line editing. Forces the decision the reviewer asks for: a scene must change plot, character, or pressure — a beautiful moral reflection alone is not enough to earn its place. Build this in Phase 2 (Architecture) and revisit it in Phase 4 (Adversarial Audit) before any developmental cut pass.

## Scene register

| # | chapter / scene | main event | POV | what changes by the end | plot thread advanced | escalates danger? | repeats a prior scene function? | verdict |
| ---: | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Verdict values: **keep**, **merge** (fold into an adjacent scene), **cut** (remove), **move** (relocate in chronology), **compress** (trim but retain).

Rules:
- every scene must change at least one of: plot state, character state, or pressure level. If it only delivers a reflection, set verdict to cut or merge.
- if two scenes share the same underlying shape (practical problem → camp argues → rule is made → moral insight lands), keep the strongest representative and merge or cut the rest.
- a scene that repeats an established motif without escalating it is a candidate for compression. Trust the established motif; do not restate it.
- when in doubt, the rule is the manuscript's own metaphor: do not build the largest possible ark. Build the one that holds.

## Function-profile audit

Group scenes by their underlying shape. Flag any shape that appears more than twice.

Common scene shapes to track:
- practical problem → argument → rule → moral insight (the "law of the camp" beat)
- procurement / logistics episode
- father-son workmanship beat
- woman-sees-social-reality-before-the-men beat
- antagonist pressure escalation beat
- sign / omen / ambiguous evidence beat
- list / count / ledger beat
- moral vignette with no plot or character change (the warning shape)

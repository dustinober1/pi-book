## Per-chapter packet contract

Each chapter packet must include:

- chapter purpose
- dominant scene engine and contrast with the previous chapter
- scene/section list
- scene-function profile: action, dialogue, exposition, worldbuilding, pressure, and pacing balance
- therefore/but causality link
- reader promise touched
- subplot, argument, or learning-objective pressure added
- external clock, midpoint, or ending pressure touched
- protagonist agency: what the lead chooses, risks, causes, or gets wrong
- secondary-character agency: whose private stake or decision changes the scene
- continuity constraints
- carry-forward constraints: specific facts, promises, reveals, tone, or obligations that must survive into prose
- tension forecast: 2-3 plausible reader forecasts plus what must stay hidden or ambiguous so the ending is not prematurely locked
- technical/plain-language load: terms introduced, terms deferred, simple model reinforced
- mode-specific safeguards: for biblical fiction/sacred retelling, source passages, invention boundary, translation sensitivity, sacred-figure handling, anachronism/modernity, miracle/supernatural policy, character humility, and sacred residue touched
- human-specificity seeds
- voice and taste-lock notes
- rhetorical-shape watch: repeated closeout, triad, negative parallelism, aphorism, or verdict-line risks
- forbidden filler
- post-draft ledger tasks

## Status values

- `blocked`: missing approval, missing outline, open quality gate, or incomplete packet.
- `queued`: ready for a bounded drafting pass.
- `drafting`: currently being drafted.
- `drafted`: prose exists and post-chapter update is needed.
- `ledger_updated`: continuity, promises, queue, and scorecards are synchronized.
- `approved`: writer approved the chapter or packet.

# Technical Seed Map Template

## Purpose

For speculative fiction, thrillers, and any book whose climax depends on a specific technical or conceptual mechanism, this artifact tracks when key concepts are introduced and whether the reader can track the logic by the climax.

The goal: the reader should understand what the plan is, why it might work, why it might fail, and what the cost will be — not only the moral logic but the technical logic.

Types: technical concept, system vulnerability, data structure, access mechanism, biological mechanism, legal provision, institutional process, physical constraint

## Seed rules

- Early seed: the reader first encounters the concept in concrete, observable form (not as a lecture)
- Middle deepening: the concept is revisited with new implications, complications, or constraints
- Late operationalization: the concept becomes actionable for the climax plan
- Climax: the reader can track both the moral logic AND the technical logic in real time

## Risk check

- if the mechanism depends on hand-waving, add one more concrete seed before the final act
- if the explanation arrives in large blocks, break it into earlier, smaller, more concrete pieces
- if a character needs to act outside a predicted pattern, the reader must understand the prediction before the subversion

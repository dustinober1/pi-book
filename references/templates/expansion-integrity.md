## Forbidden filler patterns

- repeated introspection
- duplicate exposition
- low-stakes banter
- atmospheric drift
- decorative worldbuilding
- no-state-change scenes
- thematic restatement: restating the central paradox, thesis, or insight after the reader has already understood it (trust the reader; if the scene dramatized the point, do not explain it again)
- repeated character processing beats: hand-to-pocket, not sleeping, counting, looking at screens, thinking in numbers (establish the motif, then reduce the repetition)
- explanatory commentary around strong images: if an image or object already landed, cut or compress the paragraph that interprets it
- repeated "the system is not malicious / the math is correct / the proof is a body" formulations after the pattern is established

## Audit note

- If cutting the added material would not damage plot, character, pressure, or ending conditions, it is padding.
- If a paragraph explains what the previous scene already made the reader feel, it should be cut or compressed.
- The manuscript should not increase length with repeated thematic commentary. Expansion must come from new consequence: stronger subplots, sharper reversals, added pressure, meaningful aftermath, new relational conflict, or real thematic complication.

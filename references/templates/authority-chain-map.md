# Authority Chain Map

## Purpose

For books involving institutions, interventions, platforms, healthcare, legal action, welfare checks, moderation, compliance, or crisis escalation, map who can access what, who can authorize what, and why the intervention is plausible.

## Access map

| actor | can see | can infer | cannot see | source of access |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

## Authority map

| actor | can authorize | can recommend only | can escalate to | limit on authority |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

## Intervention chain

| event | originating signal | routing path | human decision point | visible authority | plausibility risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

## Rules

- every institutional intervention scene needs a visible authority chain
- if a routed packet or context causes action, show who acted on it and why they were allowed to
- if a character has unusual access, define their professional role, limits, and evidence basis early
- if an intervention depends on layered systems, map the handoff points so the reader sees the human authority inside the machine path

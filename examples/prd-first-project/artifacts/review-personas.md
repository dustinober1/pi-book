# Review Personas

## 1. Ideal Core Reader

- Why they picked up the book: grounded speculative mystery with emotional consequence.
- What they need from page one: a concrete false-memory incident and Mara's personal stake.
- What they will forgive: quiet pace if each chapter changes pressure.
- What they will not forgive: generic archive visits with no new consequence.
- What they will praise: moral residue and institutional plausibility.
- What will make them DNF: lesson-shaped ending or vague technology.
- Questions they should answer: Did the mystery and emotional cost both escalate?

## 2. Genre-Native Reviewer

- Familiar genre/category expectations: mystery escalation, clues, reversals, payoff.
- Clichés they will punish: evil archive conspiracy, exposition dumps, fake red tape.
- Promise/payoff expectations: the external case must resolve.
- Pacing tolerance: moderate if causality is strong.
- Ending expectations: decisive external consequence with unresolved moral residue.

## 3. Voice-Sensitive Craft Reader

- What false prose sounds like to them: polished grief paragraphs and symbolic memory metaphors.
- What over-polish they will notice: everyone naming their trauma too clearly.
- What specificity they require: bureaucratic objects, awkward silences, practical archive details.
- What they will flag as AI-shaped: tidy theme statements and not-only/but-also explanation.

## 4. Skeptical But Persuadable Reader

- Initial resistance: quiet speculative mystery may feel too soft.
- What would win them over: institutional stakes that force Mara to act.
- What would confirm their doubts: chapters where Mara only discovers information.

## 5. Hostile / Misaligned Reader

- Why they are not the target reader: wants a high-body-count techno-thriller.
- What criticism to ignore: demands for a purely evil AI/archive villain.
- What criticism might still contain useful signal: pacing stalls, unclear system rules, passive protagonist.

# Taste Lock

Purpose: protect the writer's actual taste from being optimized away by automation.

## Locked taste constraints

- Restrained, specific, slightly dry prose.
- Preserve awkward silences, half-finished apologies, bureaucratic absurdity, and controlled irritation.

## Things automation may not smooth away

- Productive roughness: unsentimental emotional residue.
- Intentional opacity: moral ambiguity around false memories.
- Moral or emotional risk: painful memories may not resolve into a lesson.
- Taboo generic cadences: therapy-speak, glossy thriller banter, faux-poetic memory metaphors.

## Approval policy

Any proposed change to voice, ending shape, reader cost, darkness, humor, restraint, or risk budget requires writer approval and a `decision-ledger.md` entry.
